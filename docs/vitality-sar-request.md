# Vitality SAR draft

**To:** Data.Protection@Vitality.co.uk
**Subject:** Subject Access Request - activity points history

---

Dear Data Protection Officer,

I am writing to make a Subject Access Request under Article 15 of the UK GDPR.

I would like a copy of the following personal data that Vitality holds about me:

1. My complete Vitality activity points history, day by day, from the date I joined to the present. For each day, please include:
   - The number of activity points awarded
   - The activity (or activities) the points were awarded for, including step count, workout duration, average heart rate, workout type, and the source device or app
   - Any points that were awarded retrospectively or adjusted, with the reason
2. The configuration applied to my account that affects how points are calculated (for example my recorded date of birth or maximum heart rate, any age- or health-adjusted step thresholds, and any partner-tracker linking history).
3. A copy of any third-party activity data that has been received about me from connected trackers (Apple Health, Garmin, Fitbit, Google Fit, etc.) and used to award or adjust points.

If practical, I would prefer to receive this in a structured, machine-readable format such as CSV or JSON. A spreadsheet per data category is fine. If only PDF is available, that is acceptable.

To narrow the scope and make this easier to fulfil, I am **not** at this stage requesting:
- Free-text correspondence (emails, chat transcripts, call recordings)
- Underwriting, claims, or insurance pricing data
- Marketing preferences

If you need any of those to be in scope to satisfy the request, please let me know.

My identifying details:

- Full name: [YOUR FULL NAME]
- Date of birth: [DOB]
- Address: [POSTAL ADDRESS]
- Vitality membership number: [MEMBERSHIP NUMBER]
- Email on file: stefan@whitelabel.org

I am happy to verify my identity in whatever way you prefer.

Please confirm receipt and the expected response date. I understand the statutory deadline is one month from receipt and that an extension of up to two further months is permitted only where the request is complex.

Kind regards,
Stefan
stefan@whitelabel.org
